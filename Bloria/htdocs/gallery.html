LTW: Learning to Work at its Best

The Learning to Work (LTW) program in NYC partners community-based organizations (CBOs) with transfer high schools and Young Adult Borough Centers (YABCs) to re-engage students who haven’t succeeded in traditional settings. My role was to create engaging flyers, design research reports, and build a resource hub based on research findings.

My Role amd Tools:
  • Created flyers and infographics for research dissemination and public engagement.
  • Designed the LTW At Its Best Transfer School and YABC Reports (2024).
  • Built a Google site to house resources and tools from the research.
  • Tools: Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, Human-Centered Design, UX.
Challenges amd Solutions:

A major challenge was organizing design elements across multiple platforms (Google Docs, reports, and a Google Site) while maintaining consistency. I streamlined collaboration by creating a clear file system and ensured the right icons matched each section of the reports.

Outcomes:

The project delivered 3 flyers, 2 comprehensive reports, 5 tools for educators, and 1 Google Site that centralizes resources, helping educators better implement the LTW model. These tools have been crucial in supporting the re-engagement of students facing educational challenges.

Key Deliverables:
  • LTW At Its Best Transfer School Report 2024
  • LTW At Its Best YABC Report 2024
  • LTW Learning Resource Hub
